# Session checklist

> **Channel split:**
> - **Buttondown** — operational, for subscribers: full details, RSVP link, calendar link, discussion recap
> - **LinkedIn** — public-facing, for discovery: short teasers that point to the landing page, no RSVP link. Always end with a subscribe CTA.

## When a date is confirmed
- [ ] Add date to `sessions.md`
- [ ] Create a Google Form for RSVPs (name + email, one question: "are you coming?")
- [ ] Ask Claude to draft announcement email — provide date, time, venue, and Claude will generate the add-to-calendar link (Google Calendar URL, no public calendar needed)
- [ ] Send announcement in Buttondown (~3 weeks before, includes RSVP link and calendar link)
- [ ] Note: Buttondown automations require a paid plan. Instead, a few days before the session, manually resend the announcement to new subscribers who joined after the first send (filter by join date in Buttondown).
- [ ] Schedule reminder email — 3–4 days before (includes RSVP link again)
- [ ] Update landing page with date (ask Claude, then push to GitHub)
- [ ] Post on LinkedIn Page — short teaser: book title, rough date, one sentence on why it's worth reading. Link to landing page, not RSVP form. ("We're reading X next month in Morges — subscribe to join →")

## At the session
- [ ] Bring book suggestions for next session
- [ ] Vote or agree on next book and next date before leaving

## After the session
- [ ] Brief Claude on what was discussed → Claude writes post-session email
- [ ] Schedule post-session email in Buttondown (rich recap: highlights, themes, what divided the room)
- [ ] Update `sessions.md` with notes and next date
- [ ] Update landing page with next book and date (ask Claude)
- [ ] Post on LinkedIn Page — 2–3 sentence public recap with a hook (one thing that sparked debate or surprised people), then next book teaser and subscribe CTA. Not a summary of the email.
